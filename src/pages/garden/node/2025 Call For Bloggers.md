Hi! You're reading my blog. And when I say it's *mine*, I really do mean it. Nobody says what I can and cannot write. If I want, I can just make my <span style="transform: rotate(-0.5deg); display: inline-block">text slant ever so slightly to the right</span> and you literally cannot stop me, because this is my website and I can do and say as I please.

Right now, I write quite a bit about tech. But I could write about other things. I could pivot and become a [urinal](https://urinal.net/) reviewer, and you wouldn't be able to stop me. Because this isn't a tech blog. It's *my* blog, and I can say and do as I please.

My blog has no algorithm. When I make a post, it goes at the top of the list of posts, and people can read it or they can not and literally nothing changes either way. Also, for the people who *really* like my blog, I have this little file called an RSS feed. Their computer can occasionally check to find new posts. I can [[Is (my) RSS Dead?|guess]] how many "subscribers" I have, but I will never know for sure.

My blog has no antipatterns. It has no pop-ups, not for you and especially not for me[^1]. If I'm not ready, it will wait. And when I am ready, I will write, and the blog will embrace my words with open arms.

My blog will last. That's because it's just a whole load of static files, and static files are really easy to host. The only [incredible journeys](https://www.tumblr.com/ourincrediblejourney) it will go through are the ones I create. Of course, it will only last as long as I allow it to. There are no rules, remember? But when it disappears, that will be my choice and mine alone.

The code that makes my blog is the product of my blood, sweat, and tears. The design is mine and mine alone, bootstrapped and refined from a white page typed in <span style="font-family: serif;">Times New Roman</span> over the course of years. And it really does show in the little things. There's nothing quite like it, because there's nothing quite like me. I'm quite proud of what I've accomplished.

I know Christmas has already passed, but if you're tired of big tech this year, consider gifting yourself your own little website. It really is the gift that keeps on giving, as the blog has rewarded me for the effort I put in. Last year, I was rewarded with one million views, [dozens of amazing patrons](https://liberapay.com/e), and a job I love.

Free yourself from the painted <span style="text-decoration: #f4e613 double line-through;">yellow lines.</span>, the tracking, the ads, the arbitrary rules and follower counts and gaming the algorithm and acquisitions and shutdowns and a chronic lack of ownership. Free yourself, for the low low cost of a single domain and a moment's effort. You can start from nothing, and build whenever ready... But fair warning, it may become your new addiction!

[^1]: also, only [8kb](https://www.11ty.dev/speedlify/boehs-org/) of HTML, CSS, AND JavaScript!
