> **[[PenPen]]'s Note:** The break is "over", I think I've learned a little bit about my tendencies. I'm back on social media, my analytics server has been deliberately throttled down to take ~10s to load. I'm still working on all the stuff below, though.

Hi.

Needless to say, this week has been one of the most exciting[^1] of my life:

- On Jan 27, 2024, Google emailed me to congratulate me for reaching 50 clicks in 30 days. Today, I was congratulated to receive 6,000 clicks.
- My blog was read by 600,000 people in the past 7 days, and I'd estimate the number of people who reached out to be well over 100.
- I was [cited](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2024-3094) by the government. I was interviewed by [WIRED](https://www.wired.com/story/jia-tan-xz-backdoor/). I was [linked](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/04/03/technology/prevent-cyberattack-linux.html) to by the New York Times.
- My [Mastodon](https://social.coop/@eb) follower count has quadrupled, my GitHub tripled.
- My incredible [patrons](https://liberapay.com/e/) have proved that writing has the potential to become a sustainable source of income in the future.

This growth was immense, shocking, and quick. I cannot begin to express how grateful I am for each and every one of you. This sort of thing doesn't even happen in my wildest dreams.

The interesting thing about excitement is that if there's too much of it over too long, it becomes anxiety. I'm anxious. All this change, while incredibly electrifying, is all happening a little fast. I need a moment to process it. For one, I'm scared of the future. I'm scared I won't be able to live up to the hype. A few years ago, I was blogging about simple things like [[11ty Aliases]] and [[CSS Variables]]. Now, I'm a little worried to hit publish on things. After years of doing me, I've become obsessed with what others think, and I don't feel comfortable in my own home on the web. I need to convince myself once again that this is a space where I can write about whatever I want, be that about skiing or biking or static site generation or photography or the millions of other things I care for. Once I've done this, I am one step closer to return.

Another thing: I've lost sight of the real world. I'm susceptible to ADHD[^2], which has been in full swing since all this. It's hard to focus on things when quite literally a thousand people are watching:

![[Pasted image 20240404201119.png]]


I need time to establish clear separation between my internet life and my real one. I just need time to adjust. I need to put numbers in perspective. With all this being said, effective immediately:

- First, I will clear the backlog. I need to send thank you emails to my patrons, I need to reply to a few people. Nobody wants to be ignored.
- I will disable notifications related to this website
- My analytics will temporarily become write only. The dashboard will redirect to this page.
- My email will continue to be monitored, but I'm setting hard boundaries on how frequently I can check it. Realistically, a full detox is not realistic. I might reply to some of them.

Also, I just wanted to once again say how thankful I am. This is so cool. I'm so excited for the future. I'm so hyped. I'm so pumped. I just need a minute to get my bearings. I will return soon, as soon as it feels right for me to do so. Certainly, not before Monday. Once I return, I might keep some of these policies in some form, just a little less extreme. Once I return, if there is new patrons to thank and people to reply to and articles to update, I will. I will not, however, return until I am comfortable with the fact that there might not be.

Anyway. Just wanted to collect my thoughts. I'm sorry this is a little raw. Wait, no I'm not.
