*Previously: [[TikTok Ban]]*

For a little less than 16 hours, TikTok was disabled in the United States.

I say disabled because TikTok was never banned. The law as it was written mandated a response from the app stores, not from Bytedance itself. TikTok could've continued to operate for months to come, but it did not.

Instead, it willingly shuttered its doors, 84 minutes before the clock struck 12 in Washington. When users logged in, they were presented with the following text:

> A law banning TikTok has been enacted in the U.S. Unfortunately, that means you can't use TikTok for now. We are fortunate that President Trump has indicated that he will work with us on a solution to reinstate TikTok once he takes office. Please stay tuned!

Attempts to circumvent this page by use of a VPN were blocked, for no particular reason. Again, *there was no law requiring TikTok to geolock their app*, TikTok just did it. There is nothing overtly alarming about the messages, or the fact they needlessly presented one. Cozying up to Trump is a survival tactic, and a good one. Biden showed no interest in enforcing the ban, but making it appear that he did gives Trump room to exercise his savior complex, and TikTok is playing him like a fiddle.

But, just because he is being manipulated does not mean that he isn't winning. Now, the nation's youth all have their eyes on Trump, and as he comes to the platform's rescue years after advocating for its ban, he will win favors with them. By allowing the ban to proceed, the democrats have afforded Trump another opportunity to reinvent himself as a hero of free speech.

Now, TikTok is back. It's unclear *why* it is back, Trump hasn't even taken office yet. But *as a result of President Trump's efforts*, TikTok is back. Trump has saved the day!
