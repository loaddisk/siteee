Hello! It's a great pleasure that I can now say this with confidence, as I've recently learned people [do, in fact, read my blog](https://alpha.polymaths.social/@amin/statuses/01HPM8NBGFNX6C2X87PBY7E8X6).

Over the past few months, I've developed an app for rateyourmusic.com, below the radar. This was risky, because nobody has made an app for this website before, and it was entirely unclear how the developers would respond. Still, I wanted an app, and they say that you should be the change you want to see in the world. Why not give it a shot?

Most of my projects that start this way end half done. They end once they solve my issue, and I don't really put work in to make sure they solve the issues of others ([[For Me, Not You]]). Apple flipped the script. It turns out, Apple wants you to publish apps – you can't just quietly keep a custom side loaded app sitting around. I was faced with a decision. Should I go all in? Maybe, others might find use for this app, so I kept working. This time, I finished.

This is the first project I've brought to such a high degree of completion. The codebase sits at around 10k LOC of Swift, and another 2k LOC of Rust, and supports a wide breadth of things a music lover might want to do:

I have my own company now, [Toastcat LLC](https://toastcat.club/). Under it, I hope to make more apps and other things.
