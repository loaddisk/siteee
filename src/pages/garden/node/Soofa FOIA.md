In a [great deal of towns](https://soofa-explore-client.web.app/map), a company called Soofa has been authorized to install e-ink posters that display announcements, ads, and other things of the sort. The company *also* [collects](https://brookline.news/digital-signs-around-brookline-are-collecting-data-from-your-phone-as-you-walk-by/) [data](https://soofadigital.com/blog/soofa-pedestrian-data-collection-process) from the cellphones of unknowing pedestrians to give towns intelligence into the popularity of their streets. This form of cellphone tracking is pretty common, though it might be a little less common for a *town* to be doing it. Also, towns have been investing in people-counters for [ages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bicycle_counter), but this data is usually publicly available, and is frequently used by urban activists. By contrast, Soofa's data is not available, or at least not anywhere I can find.

I've submitted a FOIA request to the town of Brookline regarding this collection. Once I receive more information, it will be posted here. Depending on the quality of the information, I intend to send similar requests to neighboring municipalities.
