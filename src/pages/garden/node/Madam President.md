If you are not an email spammer, you almost certainly must know that I am an individual, and this is my blog. It’s been quite quiet lately, not for a lack of things to say, but for a lack of time to say them. I’ve been hard at work on a project that’s quite close to what I care about, and in due time I am so excited to discuss it here. This isn’t a technology blog, it is a place for me to share with the world what’s on my mind. It’s my home, and I am so pleased to make you my guest within it.

I also do understand that the world is not American. Statistically, for every hundred readers I receive, only 43% of them will be within the United States.  This diversity is something that I place immense value upon, and I appreciate that it must be frustrating to see American politics everywhere as a non-american. This is, however, the blog of an American, and I suppose that American politics _do_, for better or for worse, impact every corner of the world.

So with all that out of the way, I thought I would beat Silicon Valley’s venture capitalists to the punch: I endorse Kamala Harris, and I encourage all United States citizens to do the same.

When faced with a race between Joe Biden and Donald Trump, I feigned an optimistic outlook on Biden, while voicing concerns with his mental aptitude to my closest family. It has been incredibly scary to hear my peers equate the two candidates. It felt alarmingly clear that our voter turnout would be immensely low, even if some individuals reluctantly acknowledged that Biden was indeed the lesser of two evils. I tried to put on a happy face and say he wasn’t that bad, but eventually I had to begin admitting that I only said that because yes, he’s also bad, but he’s much better than the other guy.

What’s wrong with the other guy? It’s so incredibly obvious that he cares for nobody but himself. He’s a businessman with a history of fraud dated well before his presidency. Billionaires tend to have patterns of self-preservation, and the assertion that somehow he is different makes no sense. He cut taxes for the rich. He pardoned criminals, and appointed yes-men to the Supreme Court, who have now granted him full immunity for the crimes he [[The End Of Democracy|inevitably intends to commit in his second term]]. All these problems are fixed with time, but he also walked back on the Paris accord. I’m young, and he is hell-bent on fucking up the future of myself and my children for his own short-sighted gain.

No doubt, Kamala has faults, but her [climate agenda](https://heatmap.news/politics/kamala-harris-climate-record) is infinitely times better than trump. Kamala has thus far demonstrated an incredible ability to connect with the youth, as demonstrated by the success of her campaign on TikTok. She can _talk_, and she sure does bring energy. She makes politics feel accessible, unlike how it felt when it was dominated by 80-year-old men. I think she might be what this country needs, in a time of bipartisan despair. I believe that she will be able to connect to just about anybody who is willing to listen. Kamala makes me excited about the future of this country.

Please, for the sake of your children’s future, vote Kamala in 2024, and if you are able to, please take local action in your community. This is the chance we’ve been waiting for, we must make it count.
