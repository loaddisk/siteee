> [[PenPen]]'s Note: After I published this article, it was picked up by [mainstream media](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/02/25/technology/iphone-dictation-trump-racist.html), forcing Apple to issue a statement attributing the replacement to 'phonetic overlap.' What phonetic overlap????

TikTok users discovered that when you say 'Racist', Apple's voice dictation briefly flashes 'Trump' in the text field before correcting to 'Racist.' I have independently verified this behaviour. Below is a compilation of such videos:

According to Apple, dictation data is sent to them for training:

> Unless you opt in to Improve Siri and Dictation, your audio data is not stored by Apple In all cases, transcripts of your interactions will be sent to Apple to process your requests and may be stored by Apple.

Most likely, this behaviour is an artefact of such training — a machine learning model independently forming a strong correlation between usage of the two words. But it's curious. They don't sound or look anything alike, and they go in different parts of the sentence. Maybe [Simple Sabotage](https://www.cia.gov/static/5c875f3ec660e092cf893f60b4a288df/SimpleSabotage.pdf) is making a comeback.
