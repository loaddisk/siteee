I’m scared. I won’t lie. The worst scenarios keep flashing through my mind.

The election wasn’t stolen. There was no fraud. I cannot say in good conscience that trump won fair and square — the fact of the matter is he didn’t. He played dirty, telling lies and planting fear in the heart of his followers. Surely, Elon played a part. Surely Russia, like always, cast its hand. But we lost. Not only did we lose the college, we lost the popular vote. We’d rather a rapist over a woman. And somehow that is even scarier.

They have the president. They have the senate. They have SCOTUS, and they’ll have the house. They have an incredibly loyal majority of the population. There are no checks. There are no balances. Trump will be free to live out his wettest dreams.

I cannot say what will happen next with any certainty. There will be setbacks. I’m worried about foreign policy, and spiraling into war. I’m worried about my friends domestically, who could be impacted by Draconian restrictions on personal freedoms. I’m worried about our climate. I’m worried about our democracy. And also, I cannot undo the election. He won. He did.

I’m proud to say we fought like hell. We gave it our all, and now I’m tired. But we must fight. We still must fight for all that’s right. We must be strong, united, one nation, indivisible, under god.

There is still light at the other end of the tunnel. The journey there? It will be long, winding, uncertain. But we still have each other. We must walk on. We must still fight. Fight, we must. Fight.
